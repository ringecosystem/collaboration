## Completed Achievements in the Previous Quarter

In the previous quarter, Helixbox continued to focus on DeGov development while maintaining product availability and stability across the RingDAO ecosystem. The following are the main achievements:

- Successfully released DeGov `v2.0.0` and `v3.0.0`, which mainly focused on solving issues we encountered while operating DeGov Square. One of the most important improvements is the self-developed data indexing service, which helps us move away from dependency on third-party data providers and makes the data more reliable and customizable. This new data indexing service can also be used in other projects, such as the Darwinia bridge, staking applications, and other data-related products. It is now running stably for most projects in the RingDAO ecosystem.
- Added another skill to the DeGov agent skill repository, called `dao-governance-security`. This skill can analyze the security of DAO governance proposals and provide insights for users. The skill is open-source and can be found at https://github.com/ringecosystem/degov-agent-skills.
- Moved forward the proposal to unwind the relationship between RingDAO and KtonDAO. This action aims to reduce the long-term financial burden of RingDAO and improve the overall efficiency of the ecosystem. The proposal has passed and is currently waiting for the next action in the upcoming quarter. The unwinding process is planned to be completed next quarter, and we will provide a detailed report on the progress and results.
- As mentioned last quarter, we planned to focus on data services for DAO governance. This quarter, we made progress in this area. The first preview version of the data service, called DeGov Atlas, can be accessed at https://atlas.degov.ai. This service provides a comprehensive view of governance activities across various DAOs, including proposal statistics, voting patterns, and historical data. We will continue to enhance this service next quarter.
