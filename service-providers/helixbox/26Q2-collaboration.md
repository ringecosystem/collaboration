# Completed Achievements in the Previous Quarter

In this section, we will provide a detailed overview of the work Helixbox has advanced over the past quarter:

1. Steady progress was made on the token distribution and airdrops related to Crab Sunset. For more details, see [Crab Network Sunset: Final Report](https://github.com/orgs/ringecosystem/discussions/47), which lists the operations performed during the sunset process and the disposal of assets on the Crab chain. If you're interested, you can check it out. Currently, the work has been completed, and the Crab chain has stopped producing blocks.
2. DeGov officially released v1.0.0 and v1.1.0. Over the past quarter, we addressed some issues encountered by DAOs using and deploying DeGov, actively fixing them through development. By the end of the quarter, we successfully released a stable version, which is currently running smoothly on OnchainAustria and Realtoken DAO. Additionally, when releasing the stable version, we updated the DeGov website and documentation, with particular mention of the latest price model for DeGov. You can check the latest pricing model at https://degov.ai/pricing/.
3. The DeGov Market also showed some signs of improvement. When the news of Tally announcing the discontinuation of maintenance broke, it was a rare market promotion opportunity for the DeGov team. During that period, our efforts were entirely focused on external outreach. We proactively engaged with all DAOs using OpenZeppelin Governor, facing many rejections but also achieving some encouraging outcomes. Throughout this process, DeGov's visibility increased.
4. Regarding updates on DeGovX, its initial goal was to provide accurate and valuable DAO governance insights to X users and gain recognition on the platform. However, after several ups and downs in development and operation, considering X's policy impact on bot posts, it proved challenging to make it successful. Consequently, we decided to temporarily abandon this path.
5. In the past quarter, the AI Agent sector attracted considerable attention through agents like OpenClaw. DeGov is actively exploring this direction as well and has developed https://github.com/ringecosystem/degov-agent-skills. You can integrate this skill into any AI agent you prefer to access DeGov's data.

# Services Planned for the Upcoming Quarter

In the previous section, we described the current development trends of AI Agents. As the capabilities of agent coding continue to strengthen, the survival space for SaaS services is being continuously compressed. Relying solely on software development to build a competitive moat is increasingly difficult. Therefore, we have decided to pivot our product direction from governance tools to becoming a specialized data service provider focused on DAO governance. In the foreseeable future, data and data processing capabilities will be the core competitiveness of software companies.

Next, we will collaborate with the Pubfi team, a professional data integration service provider specializing in the cryptocurrency sector. DeGov will provide Pubfi with governance-related data that we have collected ourselves.

The current Degov Agent Data Platform has already integrated proposal and related forum discussion data from over 800 DAOs within Snapshot and DeGov Square. This means we can now monitor the latest governance dynamics of all mainstream DAOs in the market.

In the next quarter, we will continue to focus on building the Degov Agent Data Platform, with key efforts centered on adding new data sources and enhancing data processing and important information extraction. We aim to gather sufficient information about various DAOs and analyze it, thereby providing more valuable insights to agents or users.
